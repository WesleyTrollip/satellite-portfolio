# Core Portfolio Policy

## Portfolio Objective

The core portfolio should:

-   Generate steady long‑term growth
-   Beat inflation
-   Remain globally diversified
-   Require minimal maintenance

Target return: 4--6% annualized.

## Portfolio Size

Initial capital: €100,000

## Target Allocation

  Asset Class           Allocation
  --------------------- ------------
  Global equities       50%
  Bonds                 30%
  Cash / money market   20%

## Asset Classes

### Global Equities

Purpose: Primary growth engine.

Characteristics: - Broad global diversification - Low‑cost ETFs -
Accumulating funds preferred

Examples: - Vanguard FTSE All World (VWCE) - iShares MSCI ACWI

### Bonds

Purpose: Reduce volatility and stabilize the portfolio.

Characteristics: - Investment grade - diversified issuers

Examples: - Vanguard Global Aggregate Bond - iShares Core Global
Aggregate Bond

### Cash / Money Market

Purpose: Provide liquidity and reduce portfolio volatility.

Examples: - Money market ETFs - High interest savings accounts

## Rebalancing Rules

Rebalance once per year or when allocation deviates by more than 5%.

## Risk Constraints

Core portfolio must avoid: - leveraged products - derivatives -
individual stock exposure - sector concentration

## Monitoring Metrics

Systems should track: - Portfolio volatility - Maximum drawdown - Sharpe
ratio - Allocation drift
